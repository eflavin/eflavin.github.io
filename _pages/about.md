Eunhye Flavin is a Senior Research Scientist at the Georgia Institute of Technology in the Center for Education Integrating Science, Mathematics, and Computing (CEISMC). Her learning sciences research focuses on learning technologies and mathematics education. She received her Ph.D. from the Lynch School of Education at Boston College in 2021, specializing in Mathematics and Technology Education.

Flavin has received multiple awards and honors, including the 2026 Early Career Publication Award from the American Educational Research Association (AERA), recognition as an Early Career BIPOC Faculty Mentee by the Association of Mathematics Teacher Educators (AMTE), and the Professor of the Month Award in 2022 during her tenure as a tenure-track Assistant Professor at Stonehill College. In 2023 and 2024, she was also selected as a Senior Class Gift mentor honoree and nominated as a mentor for the Tri-Alpha inaugural induction ceremony.

She has taught courses in the College of Engineering, the College of Education, and the Department of Mathematics. Her research vision is to develop technologies and learning environments that leverage intelligent systems, such as augmented reality and artificial intelligence, to enhance mathematics learning experiences for historically marginalized populations.
